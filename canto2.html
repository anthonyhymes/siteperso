iNFERNO

an epic poem about the sins of technology

CANTO 2: LUST

The lofty and rusted Bill Gates of Hell close behind the oddball duo,

Of the inventors Alexander Graham Bell and Steve you-know-who.

Before their eyes towers a red monster bullman three times their height,

With a breath of fire, horns of steel and a long tail of iron might.

Bell speaks to the roar of the beast: “Minos we have a visitor here,

“He’s by my side, to learn the truth: the weight of his sins is not mere.”

The beast ejects flames of death from his scabbed nostrils, and scans the men.

“Humpf,” says Minos, “If you honored ‘warranties’ you’d be in heaven.

“Why can’t you fix an iPad screen? Why do I have to buy a new one?

“I got hooves! Of course I drop it! But Angry Birds is too much fun!”

“Do you have a smart cover?” Asks Jobs acting innocently “may--”

“Of course I don’t have a damn cover!” bellows Minos angrily:

“If it’s supposed to have a cover, why doesn’t it come with it?” Minos exclaimed.

“Minos, dear Minotaur,” soothes Bell, “this is part of why Jobs is blamed,

“For his part in spreading false desire and leading our souls astray.”

Minos shrugs his strong shoulders and drags his curling tail from the way.

The pair of inventors head down the path, before Jobs cracks his beak,

“You know Apple doesn’t have big margins, so---” his voice is cut weak.

They see shades of sinners who subordinated reason to desire,

Whose souls are committed to forever seek in the hot hellfire.

Bell cups his hands around his mouth, and calls to the floating souls there.

Responding, a shade comes, with a sad face and hipster facial hair.

“Restless soul, who have presented your ghostly self to us,” starts Bell,

“Can you kindly explain to Steve Jobs why you are banished to hell?”

The shade heaves an icy sigh which chills Jobs in his skin, bones, and spine.

“I’m here,” says the shade, “because I got a flu from waiting in line.

“I always had to have the newest Apple gadget, just more more!

“But it was so cold, as I stood for days outside the Apple store.

“Who would have thought that the thing which would stop me from being alive,

“Would be the microscopic improvements made in the iPhone 5!”

The shade vanishes in the swirling tornado of souls twisted.

“It is fair Jobs, that these people here, in their deaths you assisted,”

The reverent Bell continues, “They are what is known as a Fanboy.”

“God I hate that term,” mumbles Jobs, “it’s such an offensive killjoy.”

“They are what they are thanks in part to you Jobs, you created them,

“Because you continuously addressed lives as one big problem.”

“I am no more their father than you the sower of their birth seeds,

“I did not create them, I created products, which solved real needs.”

“No,” refutes Bell “You built unnecessary things, for pleasure, fun.”

“Pleasure?” Says Jobs, “Final Cut? The Mac Pro? What about that one?”

“A few of your products were truly needed, then you made others,

“New versions, colors, slimmer, it’s a wonder why anyone bothers!”

Jobs goes red: “You, Bell, confuse what you speak of with simple progress,

“Moving forward technology, that’s all, nothing more, nothing less,

“And Apple will keep moving forward driven by needs of many!”

“If that is true,” checks Bell, “how do you explain the iPad Mini?”

With no response the truth descends over Jobs and his hopes, his head.

“Come Jobs, the second circle. Follow me where you are being led...”
