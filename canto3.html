iNFERNO

an epic poem about the sins of technology

CANTO 3: GLUTTONY

The dark descent continues, the slick wet jagged path angles down,

There walk both Bell and Jobs under filthy rain burning red and brown,

Pouring in hard driving sheets of pain seeping stench in gaseous mist,

Something soft moves under their feet, and caustic words from there are hissed.

Jobs slowly looks down to find the stinking black muck in which he stands,

Is comprised of writhing souls tortured raw, with flailing arms and hands,

Which reach out from the sewage pool of rotten old status updates,

Of stale tweets, push notifications, and photos of tasty plates.

From the obscure dense fog barks Cerebus, a triple throated beast,

Who senses their physical presence near, and comes running to feast.

Bell picks up heaps of fecal Linkedin connection invitations,

And heaves them into the approaching dog's chomping mastications,

Filling the choking throats who slumps away, as they go on walking,

A blackened figure rises and addresses them quickly talking.

"Who are you, vial creature?" Begs a bold Jobs, eager to move past,

“You going to say that I’m responsible for your sorry ass?”

“I am dead, here in hell, because I could not control my desires.

“What started as limited pleasures, became what I required,

“Now I’m banished here, drowning in the alerts that I created,

“Force fed Groupon offers, happy birthday messages belated.”

“Ha!” Says Jobs, “You're not talking about Apple, but that Facebook thing,

“We at Apple don't do social media, you remember Ping?”

“He is here because his addiction to social apps took his life,

“Cutting out the real human parts of his existence like a knife,

“Eventually, he started using more and more social apps,

“Each message had at least four exclamation marks and was all caps,”

“Unable to keep their addiction from their daily behavior,

“They will be destined to live in Hell and never know their savior,”

While shaking his head calmly tells the sage Alexander Bell.

“You have still not explained that it is because of me he is in hell,”

Steve Jobs believes that he has Bell stumped, he even lets out a smirk.

“Jobs,” asks Bell “how do you think all of those communication things worked?

“They did not magically take over lives, they were enabled,

“Through the ubiquitous devices you gave the world uncabled.

“First there were text messages and emails and those little red dots,

“At the beginning there weren't too many, but soon there were lots,

“Distracting people from their lives and stealing away attention,

“Buzzing and beeping at each and every communication,

“It’s come so far, it's amazing that anyone can sleep at night!”

“At least it's a lot better than the damn blinking BlackBerry light,”

Says Jobs brashly, “people talk to each other, that's what you did too!

“How can you criticize me for doing the exact same as you?”

“Jobs, you’re self-centered, have you not noticed that I too am in Hell?

Bell scoffs and shouts angrily: “Why do you think I am here as well?

“You will learn, that we are responsible for more than what we see,

“We are also punished for our effects on the society.

“I‘m in Hell too for my sins, but what you did is far more severe,

“Which is why we still have quite a long way to go downwards from here…”
