Introduction


1000 years ago the Silk Road connected the East with the West as well as the South with the North. Lying at the crossroads: Dunhuang, the portal between the Gobi Desert and China. When Guowang, the King of Dunhaung, harnasses the power of a holy jade he leads an army to conquer all of the lands from the East to the West.

His brutality and might, plus the invincibility of the jade, bring him rapid victory. But on the return trip to Dunhuang in the middle of the central Asian steppe an assassin steals the holy jade and murders the Queen and small Prince of Dunhuang.

Suddenly without the jade, Guowang is vulnerable to attack, and in searching the steppe for the sacred gems he risks getting caught in the coming snows. He succeeds in capturing the thief but he is not in possession of the jade. The problem: the thief cannot speak or move. It is up to the remaining Prince to get him to speak and recover the jade in order to claim their position as Emperor of Dunhuang.

But life upon the steppe is never that easy...

Trapped in the vast openness between the snows of the steppes and the sands of the desert, Upon the Steppe is a novel about the treachery and brutality of the competing powers facing off on the unforgiving steppe during the time of the Silk Road and one Prince's journey to take back his rightful place on the throne of Dunhuang.
